Power Sweeping vs Manual Brushes

Most chimney companies still use the same manual rod-and-brush method that's been around for decades. Dave uses professional rotary power sweeping equipment — an electric drill-powered rotary head that spins the brush at high speed, the same gear used by commercial operators. Here's the difference:

✓ Power Sweeping Electric drill-powered — what Dave uses
  • Electric drill spins the brush at high RPM — scrubs all sides simultaneously
  • Removes hardened creosote — not just loose soot
  • HEPA-filtered vacuum captures particles before they enter the room
  • Takes 45–90 min for a thorough result
VS
✗ Manual Brushes The old way
  • Brush smaller than the flue — edges and corners missed
  • Won't shift hardened creosote — the most flammable buildup
  • No containment — soot and debris falls into your home
  • Quicker job, but less of the flue is actually cleaned
The bottom line: Power sweeping removes more buildup in a single visit, which means a safer chimney and better draw. It's standard practice in the UK and increasingly expected in Australia.
